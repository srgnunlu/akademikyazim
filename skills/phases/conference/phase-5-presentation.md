# Phase 5 — Presentation Preparation

## Phase Gate
**Enter:** Paper submitted and accepted (Phase 4 complete)
**Exit:** Presentation delivered

---

## Step 1: Slide Structure / Slayt Yapısı

Time limit drives everything. Typical conference slots:
- 15 min talk + 5 min Q&A → 12-15 slides maximum
- 20 min talk + 10 min Q&A → 15-20 slides
- Poster session → different rules (see poster checklist)

**Slide sequence:**
1. Title + your name/affiliation (1 slide)
2. Problem / motivation — why should the audience care? (1-2 slides)
3. Your contribution claim — ONE sentence (1 slide)
4. Method/approach (2-3 slides)
5. Key results/findings (2-3 slides)
6. Implications + limitations (1-2 slides)
7. Conclusion + future work (1 slide)
8. Thank you + contact (1 slide)

**Rule:** If you can't explain it in the talk time, cut it. The paper has the details.

---

## Step 2: Talk Notes / Konuşma Notları

Write `talk_notes.md`:
- Full script OR bullet notes per slide (your choice)
- Transition sentences between slides
- Emphasis points (what do you want audience to remember?)

---

## Step 3: Q&A Preparation / Soru-Cevap Hazırlığı

Write `qa_prep.md` with anticipated questions:
- Methodology challenges ("Why didn't you use X instead?")
- Scope limits ("Does this apply to Y context?")
- Replication questions ("Where is your data/code?")
- The "obvious alternative" question

For each: prepare a 2-sentence answer.

---

## Step 4: Rehearsal / Prova

- Run full talk at least twice out loud (not just in your head)
- Time yourself — stay 2 minutes under limit
- Record yourself once (video or audio) — watch it

---

## Completion Criteria / Tamamlanma Kriterleri

✅ Slides finalized and exported to PDF
✅ Talk notes complete
✅ Q&A prep complete
✅ Rehearsed at least twice
✅ Post-conference: upload paper to institutional repository or preprint server
✅ Git commit: `git commit -m "conference: [CONF-NAME] presentation complete"`

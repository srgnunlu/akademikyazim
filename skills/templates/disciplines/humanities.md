# Humanities Module / Beşeri Bilimler Modülü

## Purpose / Amaç

**EN:** Humanities research relies on close textual analysis, archival primary sources, and discursive footnotes. Argumentation is built on interpretation rather than empirical data sets.
**TR:** Beşeri bilimler araştırmaları, yakın metin analizine, arşivsel birincil kaynaklara ve açıklayıcı dipnotlara dayanır. Argümantasyon, ampirik veri setlerinden ziyade yoruma dayanır.

---

## 1. Citation Standard: Chicago 17 or MLA 9 / Atıf Standardı: Chicago 17 veya MLA 9

*   **History / Philosophy:** Default to Chicago 17th Edition (Notes and Bibliography).
    *   Enforce proper use of footnotes for both citations and discursive commentary (expanding on a point without breaking the main text flow).
    *   Use shortened citations for repeated sources after the first full citation. (Do not use *ibid.* in Chicago 17).
*   **Literature / Cultural Studies:** Default to MLA 9th Edition.
    *   Enforce Author-Page format (Smith 24).
    *   Works Cited page must have hanging indents.

## 2. Primary vs. Secondary Sources / Birincil ve İkincil Kaynaklar

In Phase 1 and Phase 2, strictly separate the source inventory:
*   **Primary Sources:** Archival documents, manuscripts, novels, historical letters, artworks.
*   **Secondary Sources:** Modern academic analysis of those primary sources.
*   **AI Rule:** The AI must ensure the core argument (Phase 2) is derived from an original analysis of the *Primary* sources, engaging with the *Secondary* sources only to contextualize or debate the interpretation.

## 3. Archival Handling / Arşiv Kullanımı

When referencing archival material:
*   Require full provenance: Collection Name, Box Number, Folder Number, Document Title, Date, Archive Location.
*   *Example:* "Letter from Smith to Jones, 4 May 1912, Box 4, Folder 2, Smith Papers, National Archives."

## 4. Close Reading & Discourse Analysis / Yakın Okuma ve Söylem Analizi

*   **Draft Generator Rule:** If drafting a section analyzing a specific text, the AI must explicitly quote the primary text and follow the quote with interpretive analysis. It must not summarize the plot without providing analytical commentary.
*   **Discourse Analysis:** If using Foucauldian or Critical Discourse Analysis, require explicit definitions of the "discursive formations" being analyzed.
